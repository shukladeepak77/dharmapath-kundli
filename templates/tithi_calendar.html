What is a Tithi?

A Tithi is a lunar day in the Vedic calendar, defined by a 12° angular difference between the Sun and Moon. Each lunar month contains 30 Tithis — 15 in the waxing phase (Shukla Paksha) and 15 in the waning phase (Krishna Paksha). Tithis govern auspicious timings for festivals, fasting, rituals and daily life in the Hindu tradition.
